# Writing for …ing: Sceneries without sovereignty

A personal repository for writing contributions to **"…ing: Sceneries Without Sovereignty"** — a collective publication by [JINN](https://jinn.pub) in collaboration with Nabarun Publication.

---

## About the Publication

*"…ing: Sceneries Without Sovereignty"* brings together nearly forty writers, artists, poets, musicians, scholars, cultural workers, and trade union activists from India, Kashmir, Palestine, Bangladesh, Brazil, Uganda, Peru, Sri Lanka, the Philippines, the US, and the UK.

The book begins from a clear position: **land is not inert or empty**. It is shaped by labour, history, memory, ecology, and struggle. Language grows from these relations. The publication is multilingual by necessity — written across Bengali, English, Kamtapuri/Rajbanshi, Kashmiri, Arabic, and Urdu. Translation here is a political commitment.

The **"−ing"** in the title marks ongoing processes — enclosing, extracting, mapping, demolishing, erasing. These are not historical events but active, continuous operations.

> "Culturalising land as heritage without struggle produces *sceneries without sovereignty*."

The book advances a grounded account of sovereignty *against* imposed state sovereignty — examining how land and language are separated through colonial rule, caste power, nationalism, capitalism, urban redevelopment, and war, and how people resist through collective struggle.

Emerged from a solo exhibition of the same name at **Project 88, Mumbai** (September–October 2025). Crowdfunded for independence, fair translation, ethical production, and wide circulation.

## About This Repository

This repository is my working space for contributions to the publication — drafts, research notes, data analysis, and visual narratives.

My approach is to bring **data and evidence** into the political and aesthetic terrain the publication engages. Data here is not neutral — it is a site of struggle too: who counts, what gets measured, what disappears from the record.

## Linked Data Repositories

Data and datasets used in this work are maintained in separate repositories and linked from [data/README.md](data/README.md). This separation keeps the writing repo clean and ensures data provenance is tracked independently.

## Themes I Am Working Within

Aligned with the publication's core concerns:

- **Land and dispossession** — data on land acquisition, demolitions, SEZs, forest rights
- **Language and erasure** — endangered language data, Hindi imposition, minority language speakers
- **Labour and migration** — internal migration patterns, informal labour, wage data
- **Ecology and extraction** — deforestation, mining, river enclosure data
- **Political violence** — UAPA arrests, demolitions, caste atrocities — making the invisible visible
